I SAW THE ANIMALS

It specifically addresses what seems to lie behind it all: a very human desire to relate to the animals from which humans emerged. It starts with an exploration on the nature of animals, including their cognitive abilities. It concentrates on mammals, the animals closest to humans on the evolutionary tree. It limits the discussion to the nature of pain and examines how animals and humans experience it. It is stated that apes have never done anything for humans and would not treat humans kindly in the wild. But humans feel a special draw to apes beyond the duty to treat animals humanely because of their genetic relatedness.
